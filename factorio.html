About This Benchmark

This exhaustive benchmark includes over 1000 CPUs from all manufacturers, including server CPUs, desktop CPUs, mobile processors, and various versions. It showcases how different processors perform in Factorio, a game that benefits greatly from high cache sizes and multiple cores. The benchmark score is derived from the PassMark CPU benchmark and has been adjusted to represent relative Factorio performance.

Analysis

This comprehensive benchmark of over 1000 CPUs from various manufacturers and types reveals several interesting points:

  • High-end server CPUs, particularly AMD's Threadripper PRO series, dominate the top of the chart for Factorio performance.
  • Desktop CPUs from recent generations perform exceptionally well, with top models competing with some server CPUs.
  • Apple's M-series chips show impressive performance, especially considering their power efficiency.
  • Mobile processors from Qualcomm and MediaTek, while not reaching the heights of desktop or server CPUs, show significant improvements in recent generations.
  • There's a substantial performance gap between high-end and low-end processors, highlighting the importance of CPU choice for Factorio players.

Conclusion

This extensive benchmark showcases the diverse landscape of CPU performance in Factorio across a wide range of processors. While top-tier server and high-end desktop CPUs lead in performance, there are many options that provide excellent gameplay experiences. The data highlights the importance of both multi-core performance and large cache sizes for optimal Factorio performance, but also shows that the game can run on a variety of modern processors, albeit with varying levels of performance. This benchmark can serve as a valuable resource for players looking to upgrade their systems or optimize their Factorio experience across different devices.
